Mohammad Alkurdi's research focuses on the security of AI-enabled systems, especially agentic AI systems that interact with browsers, tools, webpages, files, code, and external services. His background also includes adversarial machine learning, hardware Trojan detection, trusted microelectronics, and security-aware QA for regulated payment systems.

## Current Research Direction

### Agentic AI and Web-Agent Security

Mohammad currently studies why web and browser-based AI agents can be more vulnerable than standalone LLMs. This work focuses on risks created by tool use, webpage interaction, external content, long-horizon task execution, prompt injection, credential exposure, and unsafe autonomous actions.

### AI-Assisted Software and Malware Security

His research interests include how large language models and machine-learning systems can support software security analysis, malware detection, reverse engineering, and vulnerability discovery, as well as how these systems fail under obfuscation or adversarial manipulation.

## Previous Research Direction

### Adversarial Machine Learning and Hardware Trojan Detection

During his M.S. research and early Ph.D. research under Professor Fathi Amsaad, Mohammad worked on adversarial machine learning and ML-based hardware Trojan detection. This work connected machine learning, side-channel measurements, robustness evaluation, and trusted microelectronics.

### Trustworthy AI and Hardware-Oriented Security

This prior research background informs his broader interest in robust and trustworthy AI systems, especially when AI is used in security-sensitive settings such as hardware Trojan detection, side-channel analysis, embedded systems, and cyber-physical systems.

## Professional Security Context

### Financial and Payment-System Security

Before his graduate research, Mohammad worked in business-side QA/UAT for banking card and payment systems. That experience included ATM, POS, mobile POS, online payment gateway, PowerCARD-Switch, EMV-related validation, transaction routing, reversals, settlement, audit trails, fraud-sensitive scenarios, and compliance-aware testing.
