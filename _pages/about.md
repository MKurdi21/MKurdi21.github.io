I am a Ph.D. student in Computer Science and Engineering at Wright State University. I am currently working under [Professor Junjie Zhang](https://people.wright.edu/junjie.zhang).

My current research focuses on AI security, agentic AI and web-agent security, adversarial machine learning, and software/security analysis. I am especially interested in how AI systems behave when they interact with browsers, tools, webpages, files, code, credentials, and long-running workflows, and how these interactions create new attack surfaces beyond standalone LLM use.

Before my current Ph.D. research direction, I completed my M.S. research and the first two semesters of my Ph.D. work under [Professor Fathi Amsaad](https://people.wright.edu/fathi.amsaad). That research phase focused on adversarial machine learning, machine-learning-based hardware Trojan detection, side-channel data, and trustworthy AI/security systems in the context of hardware-oriented security and trusted microelectronics.

Before beginning my Ph.D., I completed an M.S. in Cyber Security at Wright State University with a 4.00 GPA and a B.S. in Computer Science from Birzeit University. My background combines graduate cybersecurity research, applied security coursework, and several years of business-side QA/UAT experience in banking card and payment systems.

My professional experience includes testing ATM, POS, mobile POS, online payment gateway, card issuance, instant issuance, and PowerCARD-Switch integrations in regulated banking environments. This experience shaped my interest in secure, reliable, and trustworthy systems where software failures can directly affect customers, merchants, and financial operations.

## Research Interests

- Agentic AI and web-agent security
- Software and network security
- Adversarial machine learning
- LLM-assisted malware and software-security analysis
- Hardware Trojan detection and trustworthy AI systems
- Trusted microelectronics and hardware-security-informed AI
- Financial/payment-system security and compliance-aware QA
- Cyber-physical and embedded systems security
