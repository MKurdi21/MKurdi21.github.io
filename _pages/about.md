Mohammed Alkurdi is a Ph.D. student in Computer Science and Engineering at Wright State University researching the security of AI-enabled systems, especially web/browser-based agents, tool-using LLMs, adversarial machine learning, and security-critical software workflows. His background combines publishable AI/security research with practical QA/UAT experience in regulated banking payment systems.

He is currently working under [Professor Junjie Zhang](https://people.wright.edu/junjie.zhang). His current research focuses on AI security, agentic AI and web-agent security, adversarial machine learning, and software/security analysis. His work is especially concerned with how AI systems behave when they interact with browsers, tools, webpages, files, code, credentials, and long-running workflows, and how these interactions create new attack surfaces beyond standalone LLM use.

Mohammed completed his M.S. research and early Ph.D. research under [Professor Fathi Amsaad](https://people.wright.edu/fathi.amsaad). That research phase focused on adversarial machine learning, machine-learning-based hardware Trojan detection, side-channel data, and trustworthy AI/security systems in the context of hardware-oriented security and trusted microelectronics.

He completed an M.S. in Cyber Security at Wright State University with a 4.00 GPA and a B.S. in Computer Science from Birzeit University. His background combines graduate cybersecurity research, applied security coursework, and several years of business-side QA/UAT experience in banking card and payment systems.

His professional experience includes testing ATM, POS, mobile POS, online payment gateway, card issuance, instant issuance, and PowerCARD-Switch integrations in regulated banking environments. This experience shaped his interest in secure, reliable, and trustworthy systems where software failures can directly affect customers, merchants, and financial operations.
